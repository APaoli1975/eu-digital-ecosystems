Digital Innovation Ecosystems in Europe

Tallinn & Barcelona (Success) vs Athens & Lisbon (Poor): Archetype Evidence for Policy

0) Introduction & Setup

Research question. Why did EU funding translate into strong digital innovation ecosystems in some regions (Tallinn, Barcelona) but not in others (Athens, Lisbon)? Our argument is that funding alone is insufficient; effective regional policies and institutional capacity mediate success.

Method. Because city/FUA coverage is patchy for several indicators, we construct two archetypes to stabilise comparisons: - Success archetype = average of Tallinn + Barcelona - Poor archetype = average of Athens + Lisbon

We present: (i) city dashboards, (ii) archetype comparisons (indexed trajectories & period averages), (iii) a composite innovation index & scoreboard, and (iv) SME finance as descriptive context. Section 7 links these quantitative findings to qualitative policy themes (EU & regional).

Data loaded. Cities: Tallinn, Barcelona, Athens, Lisbon. Window: 2000–2021.
Core KPIs: HT employment share (%), Patents per 100k employed, GERD (% GDP). VC intensity appears where available.
SME finance: descriptive only (coverage varies).

1) City Dashboards (Profiles)

Method. Within the analysis window, we require a KPI to have ≥ 3 non-missing years for a city before plotting it (3-year rule). This avoids misleading traces dominated by single points. Values may include within-city linear interpolation from the build step; no extrapolation is used.

Findings. Tallinn/Barcelona generally show higher levels and/or steadier momentum on patent intensity and HT employment; Athens/Lisbon appear weaker and more volatile. Coverage differences are noted and are part of the evidence.

2) Archetypes — Success vs Poor

Method. We tag each city as Success (Tallinn, Barcelona) or Poor (Athens, Lisbon). For each KPI×year we compute the archetype mean across available cities (require ≥ 2 contributing cities). We present: - Indexed trajectories (base = 100) for clear relative dynamics; - 3-year rolling averages to reduce noise; - Period averages (2011–2021) to summarise structural differences.

Findings. The Success archetype outperforms Poor on patent intensity and often on HT employment/GERD, with stronger momentum in indexed paths. Occasional cross-overs reflect short-run volatility rather than structural leadership.

3) Composite Innovation Index & Scoreboard

Method. For each city-year we z-score KPIs within the window and average across available KPIs (require ≥ 2). We then compute 2011–2021 city averages and archetype averages.

Findings. The scoreboard ranks Tallinn/Barcelona above Athens/Lisbon on the composite index, with a clear gap at the archetype level — consistent with structurally stronger ecosystems in the Success group.

4) SME Finance — Descriptive Context

Method. We summarise SME finance indicators descriptively (2011–2021 averages), focusing on access and cost of finance and risk capital. Coverage varies; the goal is context, not strict comparability.

Findings. Where available, higher financing costs/rejection align with weaker innovation outcomes (Athens/Lisbon), while VC intensity tends to be higher in the Success archetype — consistent with KPI gaps.

7) Synthesis & Link to Qualitative Policy Analysis

Quantitative synthesis.
1) Outcome divergence: Success archetype (Tallinn+Barcelona) outperforms Poor (Athens+Lisbon) on patent intensity, often HT employment/GERD, and on the composite index.
2) Momentum: Success exhibits stronger indexed growth; Poor shows flat or volatile series.
3) Finance context: Descriptive SME metrics (cost/rejection/VC) qualitatively align with weaker outcomes in Poor.
4) Funding ≠ outcomes: Access does not guarantee ecosystem performance without effective policies.

Link to qualitative. Next, we integrate document-coded policy themes (EU & regional) to test mechanisms: - Targeting & prioritisation (smart specialisation, digital focus)
- Institutional capacity & governance (delivery agencies, coordination)
- Complementary reforms (skills, regulation, procurement)
- Ecosystem instruments (accelerators, tech transfer, VC crowd-in)

Optional hook: if you provide data_clean/policy_thematic_tags.csv (e.g., city,year,theme,evidence_level), we can add side-by-side timelines against KPI turning points.
