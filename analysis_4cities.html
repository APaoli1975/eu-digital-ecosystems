Funding ≠ Outcomes: Why Policy Design Matters — Four EU Cities

0) Purpose & Hypothesis

Question. Does access to EU funding by itself produce a strong digital tech ecosystem?
Hypothesis. No. Money correlates with better outcomes only when regional policies (focus, continuity, implementation capacity) are effective.

Design. We compare four cities — Success: Tallinn, Barcelona; Poor: Athens, Lisbon — using a harmonised panel (Eurostat outcomes, OECD FUA macro & SME finance, and Cohesion funding). We keep charts few, large, legible, and avoid over‑plotting sparse signals.

1) Setup & Data Load

Data loaded. Cities: Tallinn, Barcelona, Athens, Lisbon. Analysis window: 2000–2021.
Core outcomes: HighTech_Employment_regional, HighTech_patents_regional, GERD_regional.

1b) Guardrails & Helpers

Guardrails. All charts below filter out series with too few points and skip gracefully if there isn’t enough overlap — no misleading spaghetti.

2) Signal, not noise — indexed momentum (big & clear)

Method. For each outcome and city, index to 100 at the first available year within 2000–2021.
Why. Levels can be scale‑driven; indexed paths show momentum (catch‑up vs stagnation).

Reading. Where coverage allows, Success (Tallinn/Barcelona) tends to show steadier upward momentum; Poor (Athens/Lisbon) shows flatter/volatile paths.

3) A compact contrast — pooled Success vs Poor

Method. Average the two cities in each group for each year (only when both cities contribute in that year), then compare.
Interpretation. Descriptive noise‑reduction; not causal.

Reading. Success ≥ Poor across outcomes in most observed years; the timing of gains differs, reinforcing that process & policy matter, not just money.

4) Funding intensity vs outcomes — does money align with performance?

Method. From the merged panel:
- eu_funding (EUR), funding_to_gdp, funding_per_emp; and FUA macro lab_prod.
We show: (i) annual payments, (ii) funding/GDP over time, (iii) scatter of funding intensity vs productivity (latest overlapping year).

Reading. Funding for Athens/Lisbon is not matched by superior outcomes. This supports the central claim: funding without effective policy design/implementation does not produce a thriving digital ecosystem.

5) A small, clear scoreboard (latest overlapping year)

Method. For each variable below, select the latest year with ≥3 non‑missing cities; z‑score within that slice.
Variables: outcomes (HighTech_Employment_regional, HighTech_patents_regional, GERD_regional) + intensities (funding_to_gdp, funding_per_emp).

Reading. The scoreboard compresses complexity into a legible snapshot aligned with Sections 2–4.

6) Policy interpretation — why the divergence?

Quant signals.
1) Indexed momentum tends to favour Tallinn/Barcelona; Athens/Lisbon are flatter.
2) Funding intensity does not correlate with better outcomes for Athens/Lisbon.
3) Scoreboard echoes the performance gap in the latest comparable year.

Policy reading.
- Success cases combine EU money with coherent regional strategies (long‑horizon digital infra, skills, public procurement enabling local tech, implementation capacity).
- Poor cases show fragmentation, weak continuity, and limited capacity — funds get spent but not translated into ecosystem outcomes.

Next step (qualitative cross‑walk). Add short, dated bullet‑timelines for instruments/agencies/reforms; align with inflection points in indexed series to make the causal chain persuasive.
